There's a growing trend in software development toward minimalism. Not just in design, but in philosophy. Fewer abstractions. Fewer dependencies. More clarity.

## Less is More

Modern development often adds layers of complexity that don't provide value. We reach for frameworks when functions would do. We create abstractions before understanding the problem.

The minimalist approach asks: what is the absolute minimum required to solve this problem? This often leads to simpler, more maintainable code.

## Tools, Not Rules

Minimalism isn't about dogmatically avoiding tools. It's about being intentional about what you add to your project. Each dependency should have a clear reason for existing.

When you think this way, your codebase becomes easier to understand, debug, and modify. Future you will thank you.
