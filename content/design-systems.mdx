A design system is more than just a collection of components. It's a shared language between designers and developers, a source of truth for how your product should look and feel.

## Beyond Components

Many design systems focus on components as their primary artifact. But the real value comes from the principles, patterns, and conventions that underpin those components.

A well-designed system reduces decision fatigue. When you need to implement a new feature, you're not starting from scratch. You're building on top of established patterns.

## The Human Side

The technical aspects of design systems are important, but the human aspects are crucial. Good documentation, clear ownership, and regular feedback loops make the difference between a system that thrives and one that's ignored.

Treat your design system as a product, not a project. That mindset shift changes everything.
